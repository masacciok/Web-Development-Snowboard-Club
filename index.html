About the Event

The event is for snowboarder to gather on the slope of Northstar resort to practice carving. Whether you are a beginner, intermediater, or high-end player, you are all welcome to join and make friends.

Snowboarding began in 1965 when Sherman Poppen created the “Snurfer,” a simple board made by binding two skis together for his daughter. In the 1970s, innovators like Jake Burton Carpenter and Tom Sims refined the design by adding bindings and improving board shapes, helping turn it from a toy into a sport. During the 1980s, snowboarding grew in popularity with organized competitions and the rise of freestyle riding, although many ski resorts initially banned it. By the 1990s, it had become mainstream, with widespread acceptance at resorts and a growing professional scene. Snowboarding reached global recognition when it debuted at the 1998 Winter Olympics in Nagano, and today it is a major winter sport known for its creativity, diverse disciplines, and strong influence from skateboarding and surfing culture.

Starting your day with a good breakfast is essential before snowboarding because it directly affects your energy, performance, and safety on the mountain.
Snowboarding is physically demanding—it requires constant balance, leg strength, and core engagement. After a night of fasting, your body needs fuel. Breakfast replenishes your energy so you don’t feel weak or tired halfway through your runs.
On the slopes, quick decisions matter (avoiding obstacles, adjusting to terrain, reacting to others). Eating breakfast helps maintain mental clarity and faster reaction time, reducing the risk of accidents.
